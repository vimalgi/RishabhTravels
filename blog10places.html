Top 10 Places to Visit in Ahmedabad by Cab

On 19, Aug, 2022




Ahmedabad, a UNESCO World Heritage City, offers a mix of history, culture, and modern attractions. Exploring by cab lets you see more, avoid parking hassles, and enjoy the city at your own pace. Here are the must-visit spots.

Sabarmati Ashram – Home of Mahatma Gandhi

Sabarmati Ashram, located on the peaceful banks of the Sabarmati River, was Mahatma Gandhi’s residence during the freedom struggle. The museum and serene surroundings make it a top spot for history lovers.

Sidi Saiyyed Mosque – The Iconic Tree of Life

Built in 1573, this mosque is world-famous for its stone latticework, especially the Tree of Life carving. It’s a paradise for photographers and architecture enthusiasts.

Jama Masjid – 15th Century Grandeur

Commissioned by Sultan Ahmed Shah in 1424, Jama Masjid is a stunning yellow sandstone mosque with intricate carvings and a vast courtyard.

Kankaria Lake – Entertainment by the Water

Kankaria Lake offers boating, a toy train, a zoo, and evening light shows — perfect for families and couples.

Adalaj Stepwell – An Architectural Masterpiece

Just outside Ahmedabad, this 15th-century stepwell is decorated with elaborate carvings and was built to store water and offer respite in the summer heat.

Science City – Fun & Learning Combined

With an IMAX theatre, robotics gallery, and aquarium, Science City is a great educational stop for kids and adults.

Law Garden Night Market – Shop & Snack

From colorful bandhani dupattas to Gujarati handicrafts, this night market is a shopper’s dream. Don’t miss the street food stalls nearby.

Sarkhej Roza – The Acropolis of Ahmedabad

This mosque-tomb complex blends Islamic, Hindu, and Jain architectural styles and offers a peaceful atmosphere for visitors.

Calico Museum of Textiles – A Fabric Heritage

One of the world’s best textile museums, the Calico Museum of Textiles houses centuries-old fabrics, intricate weaves, and traditional Indian techniques. It’s a must-visit for anyone interested in India’s rich fabric traditions.

Manek Chowk – Midnight Food Paradise

A bustling square in the old city that turns into a foodie hotspot after sunset. From pav bhaji to kulfi, it’s heaven for food lovers who want to explore Ahmedabad’s street food culture.

Conclusion

From serene ashrams and architectural wonders to vibrant markets and midnight food streets, Ahmedabad offers something for every kind of traveler. Booking a cab for your city tour ensures comfort, flexibility, and the chance to truly soak in the beauty of this UNESCO World Heritage City.
